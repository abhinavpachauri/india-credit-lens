## Section 3 — The Pipeline

### Primary Latent Asset: MSME Formalisation Cohort (2022–2025)

GST enrollment accelerated MSME formalisation from 2022 onwards. Many of these businesses now have 2–3 years of GST filing history, making them creditworthy under current bureau models for the first time. The SIBC data shows this cohort is converting: Micro & Small credit grew +43.8% over two years (₹7.18 L Cr to ₹10.31 L Cr = +₹3.13 L Cr). Medium enterprises grew +44.8% (₹2.95 L Cr to ₹4.26 L Cr = +₹1.31 L Cr).

**Pipeline math:**
- The MSME Register has ~6.3 crore registered MSMEs
- Current formal bank credit reaches an estimated 10–15% (based on credit bureau penetration estimates)
- If addressable pool = 15% penetrated with average ticket ₹25 lakh → ₹23 L Cr current portfolio
- Moving penetration from 15% to 20% = ~₹7.5 L Cr additional credit opportunity at industry level
- A lender capturing 5% of incremental flow = ₹0.37 L Cr in incremental book over 3 years

**What's blocking conversion:**
1. Absence of cash-flow underwriting models that can use GST, TReDS, UPI data without requiring 3-year ITR
2. Collateral gap — most Micro & Small borrowers cannot offer immovable property; CGTMSE coverage is limited
3. Bank branch geography — MSME credit still requires relationship banking; rural/semi-urban clusters lack penetration

## Section 4 — Risk Mispricing

### 4A. Gold Jewellery: Risk Underpriced at Current Growth Rate

Standard gold loan underwriting prices risk based on: (a) LTV relative to gold price, (b) interest rates, (c) ability to auction collateral. At ₹4.01 L Cr and growing 129% YoY, the risk concentration is building faster than lender systems can track.

**Evidence of potential mispricing:**
- 337% growth in 24 months cannot be primarily asset-price driven (gold rose ~25%)
- Volume expansion of ~4.4x implies a new borrower pool, not just higher per-unit values
- New borrowers in a gold loan product are predominantly distress borrowers using liquid collateral — this borrower profile has a different repayment behaviour than established gold loan customers
- At ₹4 L Cr and ~75% LTV, gross gold at-risk ≈ ₹5.3 L Cr. A 10% default rate = ₹0.4 L Cr gross loss before auction recovery — manageable, but growing

**Commercial opportunity:** A lender that can underwrite gold loan quality more granularly (borrower income, purpose, repayment history, geography of origin) can price more accurately and avoid the poorest-quality cohorts while competing for the high-quality ones.

### 4B. All Engineering / Manufacturing: Risk Overpriced

Engineering credit (₹3.11 L Cr) is growing +35.9% YoY, but this segment historically carried a perception of cyclical risk from the 2014–2018 infrastructure NPA cycle. Current data suggests the composition has changed: PLI-linked manufacturing has government revenue visibility, export linkages, and better working capital cycles than traditional capex-heavy engineering.

**Evidence:** The 60.3% 2-year growth is a broad-based trend, not concentrated in a single sub-sector. Electronics (+25.6%) and Other Engineering (+38.8%) are both accelerating, suggesting diversified demand rather than a single capex project cycle.

**Commercial opportunity:** A lender with legacy risk parameters calibrated to 2014-era infrastructure NPAs is systematically over-pricing supply-chain working capital and equipment finance for PLI-linked manufacturers. Repricing to current risk realised = volume gain.

### 4C. PSL Housing: Underpriced Risk Is Actually Low Risk

Priority Sector Housing (₹10.31 L Cr) growing +37.9% YoY carries government-backed demand (PMAY), affordable ticket sizes (smaller loan = lower LTV = lower absolute loss given default), and strong repayment cultures in target geographies. Yet many lenders apply a uniform spread over housing loan benchmark rates regardless of PSL vs non-PSL.

**Commercial opportunity:** Dedicated PSL Housing origination infrastructure — particularly in Tier 3/4 towns and semi-urban clusters — with a lower-cost operating model (co-lending, DSA networks, fintech partnerships) can deliver better risk-adjusted returns than equivalent commercial housing at current market spreads.

## Section 5 — Geographic Strategy

### What the data shows
SIBC is a national aggregate — geographic breakdown is not available in this dataset. However, sector composition implies geographic concentration:

- **Infrastructure credit** (Power, Roads) is concentrated in large project states: UP, Maharashtra, Rajasthan, Tamil Nadu (solar), Andhra/Telangana
- **MSME and Trade credit** is concentrated in manufacturing clusters: Surat, Ludhiana, Coimbatore, Pune, NCR corridors
- **Agriculture credit** peaks in Maharashtra (sugar), Punjab (wheat procurement → Food Credit), AP/Telangana (groundnut, cotton)
- **Gold Jewellery loans** are concentrated in South India (Kerala, Tamil Nadu, Andhra) where gold as financial asset is deeply embedded in household behaviour

### Frontier geography
The data growth rates imply the frontier is **not** in metro markets. The fastest-growing segments (Micro & Small, PSL Housing, Gold Jewellery) are semi-urban and rural products. The frontier is geographic depth — going from 25 cities to 200 cities for MSME and affordable housing, not geographic expansion to new metros.

**Strategic inference:** A lender whose branch economics require Tier 1/2 city catchments is structurally excluded from the highest-growth opportunities in this data.

## Section 7 — Structural Risks

### What the positive data obscures

1. **Household overleveraging is not visible in this dataset.** The data shows bank credit, not total household debt. The fastest-growing retail segments (gold, vehicle, other personal) are in addition to — not instead of — unsecured NBFC and fintech credit. Total household debt-to-income ratios are rising in a way this data cannot capture.

2. **The NBFC intermediation layer is opaque.** ₹19 L Cr of bank credit to NBFCs funds an estimated ₹35–40 L Cr of NBFC retail and MSME credit (with NBFC leverage). If NBFC asset quality deteriorates — as it did in 2018–19 — the contagion into bank books arrives with a 12–18 month lag. This pipeline risk is invisible in SIBC.

3. **"Other Personal Loans" (₹16.85 L Cr) is a black box.** This is the second-largest personal loan category after Housing, representing 25.1% of personal credit and growing +11.6% YoY. What is in it? Probably a mix of salary advances, top-ups on existing loans, personal loan products that don't fit other categories. The inability to decompose this number is a material analytical gap.

### Risks building now that will surface in 18–36 months

- **Gold loan auction risk (2026–27):** Gold loans originated in 2024–25 at high LTVs will mature and require renewal or auction. If household incomes have not recovered, a wave of gold auctions will create public credit event risk and potential NPA reclassification.
- **MSME first NPA cycle (2026–27):** The +43.8% 2-year Micro & Small growth cohort is in its credit infancy. These borrowers will face their first economic stress test in the next downcycle. Sectors with weaker demand (textiles, certain food processing sub-segments) are high-risk pockets.
- **PSL Agriculture Weaker Sections concentration (₹20.08 L Cr, +11.9%):** The Weaker Sections category is large and growing. If agricultural income is stressed by monsoon variability or commodity price declines, NPA formation in this category can move rapidly.

### Single most important unresolved data point before scaling

**End-borrower purpose and income for gold jewellery loans.**

At ₹4.01 L Cr and growing 129% YoY, gold is now the fastest-growing personal loan product in India's banking system. Before any lender scales gold loan origination further, the critical unknown is: what percentage of current gold borrowers are using proceeds for productive purposes (business working capital, asset acquisition) vs. consumption smoothing / distress-driven liquidity?

The answer determines whether this is a secured lending opportunity with a favourable risk-return profile, or a leading indicator of household balance sheet deterioration that will produce NPA formation at scale in 2026–27.
