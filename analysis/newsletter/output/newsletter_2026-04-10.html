TL;DR — Four things to know
  • ₹204.8L Cr total bank credit — +₹25.8L Cr added in 12 months, the largest absolute addition in this dataset. The credit cycle is genuinely accelerating.
  • Gold loans +128.8% YoY: this is demand migration, not a boom. RBI's November 2023 risk-weight increase repriced unsecured credit — borrowers moved to gold, which is secured, RBI-friendly, and still competitive.
  • Micro & Small enterprises +31.2% — three times last year's rate. The growth is real, but part of it is GST/UDYAM formalisation making previously-invisible businesses bankable for the first time.
  • Engineering credit +35.9% and accelerating for three consecutive periods. PLI commitments are translating into actual factory borrowing. This has runway.

🔎  Key Signals This Month

Three signals selected as most consequential for lenders.

+128.8% YoY
Gold loans went up 4.4x in 24 months

Loans against gold jewellery: Jan 2024 ₹0.915L Cr → Jan 2025 ₹1.75L Cr → Jan 2026 ₹4.01L Cr. Three consecutive data points show accelerating growth: +91.4% (Jan 2025), +121.1% (Mar 2025), +128.8% (Jan 2026) YoY. This is not seasonal — every quarter in this dataset shows the same direction.

Gold is emerging as the dominant collateral in retail lending. At ₹4L Cr outstanding and +128.8% YoY, it will likely cross ₹8–9L Cr by Jan 2027 at this rate. Any lender without a gold loan product is watching competitors grow 10x faster in this segment.

+31.2% YoY
Micro & Small credit tripled its growth rate

Micro and Small enterprise credit grew +9.6% YoY in Jan 2025. By Jan 2026 it's +31.2% YoY — a 3x acceleration in 12 months. In absolute terms: Jan 2025 ₹7.86L Cr → Jan 2026 ₹10.31L Cr, adding ₹2.45L Cr in a single year vs ₹0.69L Cr the prior year.

Something structural changed between Mar 2025 and Jan 2026. Whether it's UDYAM-linked formalisation, PSLC flows, or genuine new lending — Micro & Small is now the fastest-growing size segment in industry by a wide margin.

+35.9% YoY
Engineering credit growing 35.9% — capex cycle has started

All Engineering went from ₹1.94L Cr (Jan 2024) to ₹3.11L Cr (Jan 2026) — +60.5% in 2 years. The acceleration: +18.0% → +22.0% → +35.9% YoY across three successive periods. This is a consistent directional signal of manufacturing capex financing picking up.

Engineering companies borrowing for capacity expansion have long loan tenors, tangible collateral, and cash flows tied to order books. If you have the technical underwriting capability, this segment is growing 3x faster than bank credit overall.

🗺  The System This Month

Three things moved the credit system this month — and they are connected. RBI's November 2023 risk-weight increase on unsecured lending repriced credit cards out of reach for many borrowers. That demand didn't disappear. It migrated to gold loans, which are secured, RBI-friendly, and now growing at 128.8% YoY. Separately, five years of GST registration and UDYAM enrolment are showing up in credit data for the first time — businesses that always existed but were invisible to formal lending. That is Micro & Small credit growing at 31.2%, not the economy suddenly accelerating. And PLI commitments are translating into factory borrowing — Engineering credit up 60% in two years. Three different forces, three different credit markets, all moving simultaneously. This is not a rising tide lifting all boats. It is three specific tides, each with its own logic.

Gold Loan Products

₹4L Cr growing +128.8%. Digital valuation and doorstep processing are the cost advantages. MSME gold-backed products are the high-velocity entry.

The entry ticket for gold lending is low — Indian households hold ~25,000 tonnes of gold. Lenders who can do digital gold valuation and doorstep processing have a cost advantage over traditional branches.

Fintech Unsecured Book Not Visible

RBI's risk-weight action targeted fintech-originated short-tenor unsecured loans. This book is not in the RBI SIBC dataset — deterioration there would trigger another regulatory tightening.

RBI's unsecured lending guardrails appear to be biting. Credit card growth, which was 30%+ in FY22-23, has now stalled. Banks are slowing origination or tightening limits on existing cardholders.

₹4L Cr at risk
Gold LTV Risk at Scale

Most gold loans were originated near all-time high gold prices. A 20–30% price fall would breach LTV limits across the portfolio simultaneously, forcing auctions at scale.

Gold is emerging as the dominant collateral in retail lending. At ₹4L Cr outstanding and +128.8% YoY, it will likely cross ₹8–9L Cr by Jan 2027 at this rate. Any lender without a gold loan product is watching competitors grow 10x faster in this segment.

MSME Growth: Formalisation vs Real Demand

Part of +31.2% reflects newly-visible UDYAM-registered businesses, not businesses that were previously growing that fast.

Bureau checks on new MSME borrowers will return thin files. Lenders relying solely on credit scores for MSME underwriting are missing the opportunity and misreading the risk. GST data, UPI transaction history, and TReDS invoice flows are better signals.

+22.3% YoY
Medium Enterprise Lending

₹4.26L Cr growing +22.3%. Too large for microfinance, too small for large corporate credit teams. Cash-flow underwriting, ticket ₹50L–₹5Cr.

The sweet spot: businesses too large for microfinance but too small for large corporate credit teams. Ticket sizes ₹50L–₹5Cr, underwritten with cash flow rather than collateral.
