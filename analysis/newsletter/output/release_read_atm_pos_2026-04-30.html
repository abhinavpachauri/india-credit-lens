The reads that matter

Ecommerce volume share at 50.09% — holding above 50% for third month

Ecommerce represents 50.09% of total credit card transaction volume in Apr 2026, down 0.09pp from Mar 2026. Share slipped 50.17% → 50.09%, a marginal 0.09pp decline that keeps the channel above 50% for the third consecutive month and within the 28-period upper range.

So what: Ecommerce has consolidated its majority share of transaction frequency — the channel is sustaining dominance above 50% for the third straight month, confirming the shift back to online is structural, not seasonal. Lenders should treat this as the new baseline for credit card usage patterns.

ATM visit share fell to 80.84% — down 0.58pp from prior period

ATM withdrawals represented 80.84% of total debit card transaction volume in Apr 2026. Share fell 81.42% → 80.84%, dropping 0.58pp and moving below the 75th percentile (80.8%) for the first time since Jan 2026.

So what: ATM visit share is losing ground to digital channels on a sustained basis. The 80%–82% equilibrium is breaking downward — banks should expect ATM share to trend toward 79% over the next 6 months. Digital channel capacity planning should be revised upward.

UPI QR codes at 67× per POS terminal — highest ratio on record

There are now 67.3 UPI QR codes for every POS terminal in Apr 2026, up 2.66 from 64.7 in Mar 2026. Ratio rose 64.7× → 67.3×, the largest single-month jump since Nov 2025's 5.8-point surge — the gap has widened by 2.7 points as UPI QR scaled 9.6M codes while POS terminals fell 322K units.

So what: The 67× ratio confirms the two channels are on opposite trajectories — UPI QR is scaling exponentially while POS is contracting. Lenders financing merchant acceptance should allocate capital exclusively to UPI QR infrastructure; POS terminals are a shrinking asset class with no recovery signal. The ratio will continue widening as UPI QR adds 7–10M codes monthly and POS struggles to stabilise.
