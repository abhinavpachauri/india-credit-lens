## The System This Month

Three things moved the credit system this month — and they are connected. RBI's November 2023 risk-weight increase on unsecured lending repriced credit cards out of reach for many borrowers. That demand didn't disappear. It migrated to gold loans, which are secured, RBI-friendly, and now growing at 128.8% YoY. Separately, five years of GST registration and UDYAM enrolment are showing up in credit data for the first time — businesses that always existed but were invisible to formal lending. That is Micro & Small credit growing at 31.2%, not the economy suddenly accelerating. And PLI commitments are translating into factory borrowing — Engineering credit up 60% in two years. Three different forces, three different credit markets, all moving simultaneously. This is not a rising tide lifting all boats. It is three specific tides, each with its own logic.

## ⚡ What's Driving This

**GST / UDYAM Formalisation**
Post-2018 GST and UDYAM registrations created a large cohort of businesses now visible to formal credit for the first time.

**RBI Unsecured Risk-Weight Increase · 100% → 125%**
Nov 2023: RBI raised risk weights on unsecured consumer credit from 100% to 125%, raising its capital cost and slowing origination.

**Gold Price Appreciation · +25–30%**
Gold prices rose ~25–30% over 2024–25, inflating collateral values for gold-backed lending and working capital for gold-dependent industries.

**PLI Scheme / Manufacturing Capex**
India's Production Linked Incentive scheme and supply chain localisation from China are generating new manufacturing investment and bank credit demand.

**Household Liquidity Stress**
Post-COVID income disruption and elevated inflation 2022–24 increased household demand for short-tenor liquidity against existing gold assets.

**NBFC Intermediation Layer**
NBFCs funded by banks extend credit to segments banks cannot cost-effectively reach: microfinance, small informal businesses, rural borrowers.

**PSL Compliance Pressure · 40% PSL target**
Banks must direct 40% of net bank credit to priority sectors. As total credit grows at 14.6%, PSL targets grow in absolute terms — harder to meet organically.

## 🔎 Key Signals This Month

### +128.8% YoY — Gold loans went up 4.4x in 24 months

Loans against gold jewellery: Jan 2024 ₹0.915L Cr → Jan 2025 ₹1.75L Cr → Jan 2026 ₹4.01L Cr. Three consecutive data points show accelerating growth: +91.4% (Jan 2025), +121.1% (Mar 2025), +128.8% (Jan 2026) YoY. This is not seasonal — every quarter in this dataset shows the same direction.

*Gold is emerging as the dominant collateral in retail lending. At ₹4L Cr outstanding and +128.8% YoY, it will likely cross ₹8–9L Cr by Jan 2027 at this rate. Any lender without a gold loan product is watching competitors grow 10x faster in this segment.*


### +31.2% YoY — Micro & Small credit tripled its growth rate

Micro and Small enterprise credit grew +9.6% YoY in Jan 2025. By Jan 2026 it's +31.2% YoY — a 3x acceleration in 12 months. In absolute terms: Jan 2025 ₹7.86L Cr → Jan 2026 ₹10.31L Cr, adding ₹2.45L Cr in a single year vs ₹0.69L Cr the prior year.

*Something structural changed between Mar 2025 and Jan 2026. Whether it's UDYAM-linked formalisation, PSLC flows, or genuine new lending — Micro & Small is now the fastest-growing size segment in industry by a wide margin.*


### +35.9% YoY — Engineering credit growing 35.9% — capex cycle has started

All Engineering went from ₹1.94L Cr (Jan 2024) to ₹3.11L Cr (Jan 2026) — +60.5% in 2 years. The acceleration: +18.0% → +22.0% → +35.9% YoY across three successive periods. This is a consistent directional signal of manufacturing capex financing picking up.

*Engineering companies borrowing for capacity expansion have long loan tenors, tangible collateral, and cash flows tied to order books. If you have the technical underwriting capability, this segment is growing 3x faster than bank credit overall.*

## 🎯 Where to Play

**Gold Loan Products** · +128.8% YoY
Gold loans grew +91.4% (Jan 2025), +121.1% (Mar 2025), +128.8% (Jan 2026) — the growth rate is itself accelerating, not decelerating. At ₹4.01L Cr today, this segment is still smaller than Vehicle Loans (₹7.2L Cr). The headroom is large.
*The entry ticket for gold lending is low — Indian households hold ~25,000 tonnes of gold. Lenders who can do digital gold valuation and doorstep processing have a cost advantage over traditional branches.*

**Medium Enterprise Lending** · +22.3% YoY
Medium enterprise credit at ₹4.26L Cr is growing at +22.3% YoY but is only 10% of total industry credit. At current growth rates, it doubles in under 4 years. These businesses have GST records, some bureau history, and genuine working capital and capex needs.
*The sweet spot: businesses too large for microfinance but too small for large corporate credit teams. Ticket sizes ₹50L–₹5Cr, underwritten with cash flow rather than collateral.*

**Engineering Supply Chain Credit** · +35.9% YoY
All Engineering went from ₹1.94L Cr (Jan 2024) to ₹3.11L Cr (Jan 2026) — +60.5% in 2 years. The acceleration: +18.0% → +22.0% → +35.9% YoY across three successive periods. This is a consistent directional signal of manufacturing capex financing picking up.
*Engineering companies borrowing for capacity expansion have long loan tenors, tangible collateral, and cash flows tied to order books. If you have the technical underwriting capability, this segment is growing 3x faster than bank credit overall.*

**PSL Affordable Housing — Tier 3/4** · +37.9% YoY
PSL housing growing +37.9% is the strongest combination in this data: government demand support via PMAY, small loan sizes meaning lower loss if something goes wrong, and strong repayment behaviour in the towns where most of this demand sits. The constraint isn't demand — it is distribution. Very few lenders have origination infrastructure in Tier 3 and 4 cities.
*Building or partnering for origination in 50 targeted semi-urban clusters — through agents, business correspondents, or co-lending with housing finance companies — gets you to where the demand is. You earn both the business and PSL credit certificates.*

**IT / Software Working Capital** · +20.7% YoY
Computer Software grew from ₹0.263L Cr (Jan 2024) to ₹0.407L Cr (Jan 2026) — +54.8% in 2 years. YoY: +28.2% (Jan 2025), +27.0% (Mar 2025), +20.7% (Jan 2026). At ₹0.41L Cr, this is tiny relative to sector size — India's IT sector revenue is ₹22L Cr+ annually, yet bank credit is ₹0.41L Cr.
*IT companies are underleveraged relative to their cash flows. The opportunity is in structured working capital, acquisition financing, and employee stock loan programs — not traditional collateral-based lending.*

**PSLC Origination — Sell Certificates** · None
Weaker Sections (incl. PSLC-SF/MF): ₹15.89L Cr (Jan 2024) → ₹20.08L Cr (Jan 2026), +26.3% in 2 years. YoY: +12.9% (Jan 2025), +15.3% (Mar 2025), +11.9% (Jan 2026). The PSLC component means banks are actively buying certificates to meet targets — and the underlying microfinance/small farmer credit is growing regardless.
*Lenders that can originate in weaker sections directly — microfinance, NBFC-MFIs, SFBs — are both serving a growing segment and generating PSLC inventory they can sell to banks that can't reach these borrowers.*

## ⚠️ What Could Break

**Gold LTV Risk at Scale · ₹4L Cr at risk**
Most gold loans were originated near all-time high gold prices. A 20–30% price fall would breach LTV limits across the portfolio simultaneously, forcing auctions at scale.

**NBFC Concentration in Bank Funding · ₹19L Cr, +17.8%**
₹19.05L Cr of bank exposure growing +17.8% annually. Top 20 NBFCs are likely 80%+ of this. A single large NBFC stress event triggers contagion across bank credit.

**Fintech Unsecured Book Not Visible**
RBI's risk-weight action targeted fintech-originated short-tenor unsecured loans. This book is not in the RBI SIBC dataset — deterioration there would trigger another regulatory tightening.

## 🔍 What We Can't See

**Shipping: Reclassification, Not Collapse · -98.6%**
₹7.3L Cr → ₹0.1L Cr is a reporting change, not a credit contraction. Series is unusable until restated.
*Ignore all Shipping YoY figures until the series is restated. Do not use Shipping data from Jan 2026 for any analysis.*

**MSME Growth: Formalisation vs Real Demand**
Part of +31.2% reflects newly-visible UDYAM-registered businesses, not businesses that were previously growing that fast.
*Bureau checks on new MSME borrowers will return thin files. Lenders relying solely on credit scores for MSME underwriting are missing the opportunity and misreading the risk. GST data, UPI transaction history, and TReDS invoice flows are better signals.*

**PSL Lines Overlap — Do Not Sum**
PSL sub-categories are not separate buckets. The same loan can appear in multiple PSL lines. Adding them overstates the total.
*Only the overall PSL target (40% of net bank credit) has legal meaning. Never add up the PSL sub-categories to estimate the total opportunity — the number you get will be much larger than the real pool.*

**Multiple Series Broke in 2025 · 4 series affected**
Shipping (Jan 2026), Renewable Energy (Mar 2025), Social Infrastructure (Mar 2025), and Advances against shares (Mar 2025) all show discontinuities that make YoY comparisons meaningless.
*Ignore all Shipping YoY figures until the series is restated. Do not use Shipping data from Jan 2026 for any analysis.*

**NBFC Retail Lending Not Visible Here**
The ₹19.05L Cr is bank credit TO NBFCs (wholesale). What NBFCs lend to end borrowers — microfinance, small business, gold — is not in this dataset.
*When NBFC credit grows, it's bank money flowing to end borrowers through a middleman. For banks without retail reach, NBFCs are their route to segments they can't serve directly. For NBFCs, cheap bank funding is the input that makes their model viable.*
