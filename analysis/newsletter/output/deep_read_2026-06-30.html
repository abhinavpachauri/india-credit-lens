The cross-system reads

Cross-system signal: credit_card flow leading stock — origination headroom

Credit card spending is slowing down, which usually happens before credit card loans start shrinking. When people spend less on their cards, they typically pay down their balances over the next few months, causing total credit card debt to drop. This gives banks a few months' head start to see what's coming.

So what: Banks should prepare for lower credit card loan growth and adjust their lending plans before the slowdown hits their loan books.

Unsecured retail credit appetite — expanding (5/5 measurements observed)

Personal loans for credit cards and other purposes grew 3.78 percent and 12.7 percent year-on-year, while credit card balances outstanding rose 8.15 percent. Consumer durable loans fell 3.38 percent. Most unsecured lending is expanding, meaning banks are willing to lend more to people without collateral because they see demand and acceptable risk.

So what: A bank should grow its unsecured personal loan book now, especially in the other personal loan category growing at 12.7 percent, while watching credit quality closely since these loans have no asset backing.

Card-based consumption activity — expanding (5/5 measurements observed)

Card terminals grew 1.45 percent, vehicle loans jumped 17.97 percent, but consumer durable loans fell 3.38 percent. People are borrowing heavily to buy cars and trucks, using cards a bit more, yet pulling back on financing fridges and TVs. The pattern says households are spending selectively—big-ticket transport yes, home goods no.

So what: A bank should push auto finance hard, keep card offers steady, and avoid chasing consumer-durable portfolios until that 3.38 percent decline reverses.

How this read is computed:

•  Card-based consumption activity → expanding

Live openings — Credit

Co-lending & warehouse-financing infrastructure

Small business credit is growing at 26.18 percent this year and now makes up 23.06 percent of all business lending. Growth in micro and small business lending jumped by 23.77 percentage points, driven by government systems like UDYAM registration and GST data that make it easier to check if tiny businesses are real and creditworthy. Banks cannot reach thousands of small borrowers alone, so they need partners who can do the ground work of finding customers and verifying them.

So what: Banks should build partnerships with fintech companies and NBFCs who specialize in finding and checking small borrowers, using co-lending or warehouse financing to fund these loans while the partners handle customer acquisition and verification.

Gold loan market entry / deepening for banks

Banks are growing gold loans by 105.5 percent compared to last year, and this growth has been climbing for 12.0 periods in a row. Gold prices jumped sharply between 2023 and 2026, so customers can now borrow much more money using the same gold jewelry or coins as security. Banks are winning customers from finance companies because they have local branches where staff can check the gold is real and store it safely, plus they charge lower interest rates.

So what: The bank should open more gold loan counters in branches, train staff to test gold quickly, and advertise lower rates than finance companies to pull in customers who want bigger loans against their existing gold.

MSME origination infrastructure investment window

Small business lending is growing at 32.68 pct per year, and these tiny businesses now make up 23.06 pct of all MSME lending. Their growth rate jumped by 23.77 pp recently, while bigger MSME borrowers are growing much slower—the gap between small and large is 11.8 pp. Banks can now check if these small businesses can repay loans using government registration systems and GST records, which makes lending to them safer and faster.

So what: A bank should invest in systems that connect to UDYAM registration and GST data so it can quickly approve loans for small businesses that were previously too hard to assess.

Live openings — Payments

Credit card penetration deepening — PSB share gap is the opening

India has 120454115.0 credit cards outstanding, growing at 8.47 percent year-on-year. Private banks hold 70.91 percent of these cards, while public sector banks hold only 23.83 percent — even though public banks control about 64 percent of the debit card base. This gap means public banks have millions of existing customers with debit cards who could be upgraded to credit cards at much lower cost than finding brand-new customers.

So what: Public sector banks should launch targeted upgrade programs offering credit cards to their existing debit card holders, capturing share in a growing market where they are currently underrepresented.

Rural cash distribution via Micro ATM / Business Correspondent networks

India has 1358241.0 micro ATMs, but the number fell -8.11 percent recently. These small cash machines work in villages and towns where a full bank branch or big ATM costs too much to run. Even though the count dropped, people in these areas still need cash every day, and micro ATMs cost far less to set up than a real branch.

So what: A bank should keep or grow its network of micro ATMs and business correspondents in rural areas because customers there depend on cash and this channel costs much less than building branches.

Embedded transit and tap-to-pay payment products

Credit card spending in the 'other' category jumped 2365.96 percent in value and 714.01 percent in volume year-on-year. This means people are using cards in completely new places — likely transit gates, vending machines, or small merchants that just got tap-to-pay terminals. The growth is so extreme it shows a new habit forming, not just existing users spending more.

So what: Banks should rush to issue cards that work on these new payment rails (transit tap or UPI-linked credit) and sign up the merchants or transit systems rolling out the terminals, because whoever gets in first will own the new spending habit.

Watch-outs

Infrastructure repayment wave with insufficient new origination pipeline

Banks over-indexed to traditional infrastructure credit face a structural squeeze: the FY22-24 project book moves to repayment (compressing balances) while the next-cycle pipeline (renewables, data centres) is slower to ramp. Banks without next-cycle project finance capability face credit portfolio contraction.

So what: Move on this while the trend is still running in your favour.

CIC reporting compliance gaps creating off-balance-sheet credit risk

RBI Digital Lending Guidelines (2022/2025) require BNPL and digital credit to report to Credit Information Companies. However, compliance may be incomplete among newer digital lenders operating under first-loss guarantee structures or co-lending arrangements where the regulated entity's on-book exposure is partially shielded. If reporting gaps exist, the true unsecured credit stress is understated in bank balance sheets and in SIBC data.

So what: Move on this while the trend is still running in your favour.

Credit card market concentrated in top-5 private banks

The top-5 private banks control approximately 71% of credit card outstanding. The largest single issuer holds ~20% of the market. This concentration creates systemic fragility — as demonstrated when a single payment bank's exit caused a market-wide debit card step-down. Similar disruption from a major credit card issuer would have proportionally larger effects.

So what: Move on this while the trend is still running in your favour.
