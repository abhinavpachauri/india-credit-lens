March 2026: FY26 Closes. Everything Confirmed.

Issue #3  ·  2026-05-02  ·  by Abhinav Pachauri  ·  indiacreditlens.com

Every year the RBI publishes a March year-end SIBC — the full fiscal year picture in one file. India Credit Lens now has three years of data merged into a single series. Issue #3 is the FY-end confirmation: every signal we tracked this year now has a final number.

₹213.6L Cr. +16.1% YoY. FY26 closes as the strongest credit year since FY22 — and every major signal we tracked came in stronger than the interim reads suggested. MSME growth tripled from FY25 to FY26. Gold loans confirmed a 5× move over two years. The NBFC channel swung from +7.4% to +26.3% in a single year. And for the first time in this dataset, all four main sectors — industry, services, personal loans, and agriculture — accelerated simultaneously. The FY-end file doesn't introduce new stories. It closes the ones we've been tracking and sets the stakes for FY27.

TL;DR

  • ₹213.6L Cr total bank credit. +16.1% YoY. FY26 is the strongest credit expansion year since FY22.
  • MSME confirmed at +33.1% YoY — 4× the FY25 growth rate. PSL Micro & Small independently corroborates at +29.5%. Two series, same direction, both confirmed at FY-end.
  • Gold loans: ₹0.93L Cr → ₹4.60L Cr in two years. The reclassification stock effect is fully recognised. FY27 growth will be flow-only — real disbursements, no more one-time tailwinds.
  • All four main sectors accelerated simultaneously for the first time in this dataset. The 2023-24 policy tightening cycle is fully absorbed. FY27 will test whether this is a structural expansion or a catch-up cycle.

🗺 The System This Month

Three signals we tracked across Issues #1 and #2 now have their FY-end numbers, and all three came in stronger. MSME went from an interim +31% to a confirmed +33.1% at year-end — the 4× acceleration over FY25 is not a base-period artefact. Gold loans closed at ₹4.60L Cr, a 5× move from March 2024, but the March 2026 file also confirms the reclassification stock effect is complete — FY27 gold loan growth will be a clean read on real demand. The NBFC channel reopened sharply: +26.3% FY-end after +7.4% in FY25, nearly 4× the FY25 incremental in a single year. What makes this FY-end file distinctively different from the interim reads is the synchronisation signal: every main sector — industry, services, personal loans, agriculture — accelerated in FY26. That has not happened once in the two prior years of this dataset. Whether that synchronisation holds into FY27 is the only question that matters now.

📡 The Signals

★ The Synchronisation Signal

New this issue

+16.1% YoY system — Industry +14.8%, Services +13.4%, Personal Loans +16.4%, Agriculture +16.0%

All four main sectors accelerated simultaneously — a first in this dataset

The March 2026 FY-end file closes FY26 at ₹213.6L Cr (+16.1% YoY) — the strongest credit year since FY22. But the more significant signal is the synchronisation: all four main sectors accelerated in FY26. In FY24 and FY25, personal loans ran far ahead while industry lagged. In FY26, industry reaccelerated, services reached its highest YoY in the dataset, and agriculture moved to +16.0% — all while personal loans held above 16%. This degree of broad-based acceleration has not appeared once in the Jan 2024–Mar 2025 data. It either marks a genuine structural expansion — formalisation, PLI capex, NBFC channel reopening all converging — or the leading edge of a credit cycle that will produce stress in FY27-28. The FY-end confirmation doesn't resolve that question; it makes it urgent.

Any credit portfolio that underperformed 16% system growth in FY26 has lost market share during the system's strongest expansion year. The FY27 question: does synchronised acceleration reflect structural demand, or will first-cycle borrower stress and NBFC re-tightening compress the growth rate?

★ The MSME Scale Confirmation

New this issue

Micro & Small +33.1% YoY (₹10.73L Cr) | PSL MSE +29.5% FY — both confirmed at FY-end

MSME credit growth tripled from FY25 to FY26 — confirmed by two independent series

FY26 closes with Micro & Small credit at +33.1% YoY — against +9.6% in FY25. The FY26 incremental alone is ₹2.65L Cr, four times the ₹0.63L Cr FY25 add. The PSL Micro & Small series confirms independently at +29.5% FY. Two measurement approaches, both confirmed at year-end, both pointing to the same structural acceleration. The driver: GST-registered MSMEs from 2019-22 now have 4-6 years of digital financial history — enough for alt-data underwriting models to run. A meaningful share of this growth is first-cycle formal borrowers with thin or blank bureau histories. Bureau-only underwriting cannot scale to this book. FY27-28 will be the first stress test for alt-data models as the first-cycle borrower loss curves begin to emerge.

The MSME formalisation wave is confirmed at scale. The window for alt-data underwriting model training is now — GST + UPI + e-way-bill data is 4+ years deep. FY27 growth will be the first real test: can disbursement velocity hold above 20% when the base is now ₹10.73L Cr instead of ₹8.08L Cr?

✅ MSME origination infrastructure

Banks with MSME origination infrastructure built in 2022-24 are now harvesting. The window is open but will close as competition intensifies. The next ₹10L Cr of industrial credit will come from SMEs.

Lenders who build alternative underwriting before bureau coverage fills in will have 2-3 years of pricing advantage. FY27-28 will be the first stress test for these alt-data models. Loss curves could surprise — first-cycle borrowers have no through-the-cycle performance history.

★ The NBFC Channel Reopens

New this issue

₹20.66L Cr (+26.3% FY-end) — FY26 incremental ₹4.30L Cr vs ₹1.12L Cr in FY25

Bank credit to NBFCs nearly 4× the FY25 incremental in a single year

Bank credit to NBFCs closed FY26 at ₹20.66L Cr, up from ₹16.35L Cr in Mar 2025 — a 26.3% FY move and nearly 4× the FY25 incremental. The 2023 risk-weight tightening cycle that compressed NBFC funding through FY25 is fully absorbed. The reopening is confirmed at FY-end, not just in interim months. The bank-NBFC relationship is now the fastest-growing major services sub-category, overtaking the prior year's infrastructure-led services composition. The infrastructure wedge: banks need NBFC origination reach in MSME, gold, and vehicle lending; NBFCs need bank balance sheet capacity. Co-origination agreements, warehouse lines, and loan-management systems with co-lending support are the product wedge.

The NBFC funding channel is fully open. Fintechs and NBFCs with bank co-lending agreements are now running on the system's fastest-growing funding source. The risk: RBI could re-tighten risk weights if it perceives NBFC overheating — watch H1 FY27 FSR.

📅 What to Watch Next

Next release: May/June 2026 (RBI SIBC Jan 2027 — first FY27 interim read)

  • Gold loan FY27 disbursements — flow-only growth after two years of stock effects. Can the underlying demand sustain above 30%? Gold price reversal >20% is the primary risk — LTV ratios on marginal loans are already aggressive.
  • MSME first-cycle borrower loss curves — FY26 onboarded a wave of first-cycle formal borrowers with thin bureau histories. FY27 is the first vintage year where delinquency data will emerge. Alt-data underwriting models face their first real test.
  • NBFC risk-weight re-tightening — the FY26 reopening was sharp (+26.3%). RBI monitors NBFC systemic risk closely. Watch H1 FY27 Financial Stability Report for any re-tightening signal.
  • Renewable energy project finance drawdowns — the FY27-28 infrastructure credit wave. Watch for any Infrastructure sub-sector crossing +15% YoY in the next SIBC as PPA-to-drawdown timelines mature.
