PSL Housing +38%: a number that looks like growth but isn't

Issue #1 read: Issue #1 flagged the +37.9% surge as suspicious — it reversed two consecutive flat periods with no visible demand catalyst. Three explanations were offered (PMAY acceleration, reclassification, genuine new origination) but left unresolved.

Updated: Confirmed reclassification. The Oct 2024 RBI revision raised PSL housing loan ceilings (metro: ₹35L → ₹45L, non-metro: ₹25L → ₹35L). Loans already on bank books that previously exceeded the old limits now qualify as PSL. The ₹2.87L Cr appearing as credit growth is existing exposure being relabelled — not new lending.

PSL housing achievement figures for FY26 are not comparable to prior years. Any lender or analyst citing +38% PSL housing growth as evidence of market expansion is reading a classification event as an economic signal. The affordable housing opportunity is real — PMAY, Tier 3/4 distribution gaps, strong repayment behaviour — but this particular number cannot be used to size it.
